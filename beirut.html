Two Days, Two Attacks

Beirut

In Beirut, on November 12th, 2015, there were two suicide bombings in the marketplace of Bourj el-Barajneh, only a few minutes apart and several meters apart. A third man was discovered with explosives on him, but he was killed, most likely by one of the other blasts, before he could detonate his vest. 43 were killed in the attack and more than 200 were injured.

Media

Only 11% of the top 500 news sources had a story on it within 24 hours according to Huffington Post, and 443 total news sources had covered it within 30 hours. Many articles written about it were written after the Paris attack, comparing the two assaults.

Public

The hashtags PrayforBeirut, BeirutAttacks and Beirut spiked at the same time as the Paris hashtags. Most people did not even know about the Beirut bombing until seeing it compared to Paris on their social media feeds. The trends for Beirut came about as people noticed that this similar tragedy had hardly been mentioned on their social media.
